📖 Vocabulary #1

The Language of Media & Communication
12 words · Cambridge Dictionary Definitions · Visual Examples
🎤 Speaking Unit · Feb 10 – Mar 5
💡 How to study this vocabulary: Look at the illustration, read the definition and study the example sentence. Then try to write your own sentence for each word. You'll be tested on these in the weekly dictation!
1
Article
noun
▼

a piece of writing on a particular subject in a newspaper, magazine, or on a website.

Example · I read an interesting article about climate change in the newspaper.
2
Blogger
noun
▼

a person who writes a blog (= a record of your thoughts that you put on the internet for other people to read).

Example · She became a famous blogger after writing about her travels around the world.
3
Headline
noun
▼

a title printed in large letters above a story in a newspaper, or the main points of the news that are broadcast on television or radio.

Example · The president's resignation was the headline on every newspaper.
4
Interview
noun
▼

a meeting in which someone asks another person questions, especially for a newspaper or television programme, or for a job.

Example · The reporter conducted an interview with the award-winning author.
5
News / The News
noun
▼

information or reports about recent events, broadcast on television or radio, or published in newspapers.

Example · She watches the news every evening to stay informed about world events.
6
Paper
noun
▼

a newspaper.

Example · He picks up a paper every morning on his way to work.
7
Report
noun
▼

a written or spoken description of a situation, event, or problem, usually made after careful consideration of the facts.

Example · The journalist wrote a detailed report about the flooding in the city.
8
Reporter
noun
▼

someone whose job is to discover information about news events and describe them for a newspaper, magazine, television, or radio.

Example · The reporter rushed to the scene of the accident to cover the story.
9
Review
noun
▼

a report in a newspaper, magazine, or programme that gives an opinion about a new book, film, television programme, etc.

Example · The film received excellent reviews from critics around the world.
10
Disinterest
noun
▼

lack of interest in something or someone.

Example · His disinterest in the topic was obvious from the way he looked out the window.
11
Timeline
noun
▼

a plan that shows the times at which particular events are expected to happen, or a representation of the order in which things happened or will happen.

Example · The teacher drew a timeline on the board to show the key events of World War II.
12
Research
noun
▼

a detailed study of a subject, especially in order to discover new information or reach a new understanding.

Example · She spent months doing research for her article on ancient civilizations.

📖 Vocabulary #2

The Language of Time & Sequence
11 words · Cambridge Dictionary Definitions · Time Conjunctions
⏱️ Grammar Unit · Time Connectors
💡 How to study this vocabulary: These words connect two actions or events in time. Study the meaning, then look at the example sentence and try to write your own. Pay attention to which action comes first and which comes second!
1
After
conjunction
▼

following in time; at a later time than something else.

Example · She went to bed after she finished her homework.
2
As
conjunction
▼

at the same time that another event is happening; while.

Example · He listened to music as he walked to school.
3
As soon as
conjunction
▼

immediately when something happens; at the moment that.

Example · As soon as the bell rang, the students ran out of the classroom.
4
Before
conjunction
▼

earlier in time than something else.

Example · You should always wash your hands before you eat.
5
By the time
conjunction
▼

when something has happened or will have happened; not later than when.

Example · By the time he arrived at the party, everyone had already left.
6
Every time
conjunction
▼

each time that something happens; on each occasion that.

Example · Every time it rains, she takes her umbrella to school.
7
Since
conjunction
▼

from a time in the past until now; continuously from a past event to the present.

Example · She has lived in this city since she was a child.
8
Until
conjunction
▼

up to the point in time or the event mentioned; continuing up to a certain time.

Example · They played outside until it got dark.
9
When
conjunction
▼

at the time that; at or during the time that an event happens.

Example · When I grow up, I want to be a teacher.
10
Whenever
conjunction
▼

every time or at any time that something happens; no matter when.

Example · Whenever she feels sad, she calls her best friend.
11
While
conjunction
▼

during the time that something else is happening; at the same time as a longer action or situation.

Example · Mom was cooking dinner while Dad was watching TV.

Use Cambridge Dictionary online to listen to the pronunciation. Then use at least 5 of these conjunctions to write sentences about your daily routine!

📖 Vocabulary #3

Personal Qualities
10 words · Cambridge Dictionary Definitions · Character Traits
🌟 Describing People · Personality
💡 How to study this vocabulary: These words describe personality traits. Study each definition and example, then try to describe yourself or someone you know using at least 5 of these words!
1
Determined
adjective
▼

having a strong will to do something and not allowing anyone or anything to stop you.

Example · She was determined to pass all her exams, so she studied every day.
2
Easy-going
adjective
▼

relaxed and not easily worried or upset; happy to accept things as they are.

Example · My teacher is very easy-going — she never gets angry about small mistakes.
3
Hardworking
adjective
▼

always putting a lot of effort and energy into your work.

Example · He is such a hardworking student that he stays in the library every afternoon.
4
Impatient
adjective
▼

wanting something to happen soon; easily annoyed by delays or mistakes.

Example · She became impatient while waiting for the bus that was one hour late.
5
Motivated
adjective
▼

feeling enthusiastic and eager to do something; having a reason to act.

Example · The coach kept the team motivated by celebrating every small victory.
6
Passionate
adjective
▼

having or showing very strong feelings or enthusiasm about something.

Example · She is passionate about music and practises the guitar for hours every day.
7
Shy
adjective
▼

nervous or embarrassed in the company of other people; not liking to talk or be noticed.

Example · He was so shy that he never raised his hand in class, even when he knew the answer.
8
Sociable
adjective
▼

enjoying being with other people; friendly and easy to talk to.

Example · Maria is very sociable and always makes new friends at school events.
9
Strict
adjective
▼

demanding that rules are obeyed; not allowing much freedom.

Example · Our maths teacher is very strict — she never accepts homework late.
10
Talented
adjective
▼

having a natural ability to do something well.

Example · He is a talented artist who won first prize in the school drawing competition.

📖 Vocabulary #4

Phrasal Verbs: Making Progress
9 phrasal verbs · Cambridge Dictionary Definitions · Visual Examples
⚡ Phrasal Verbs · Verb + Particle
💡 What is a phrasal verb? A phrasal verb is a verb + a particle (preposition or adverb) that together create a new meaning. For example: give ≠ give up! Study each one carefully.
1
Bring together
phrasal verb
▼

to cause people to meet or be united, especially to work on something or achieve a goal.

Example · The project helped to bring together students from different schools.
2
Count on
phrasal verb
▼

to depend on someone or expect something to happen; to trust or rely on.

Example · You can always count on her — she never lets her friends down.
3
Get along
phrasal verb
▼

to have a friendly relationship with someone; to be on good terms.

Example · The two brothers didn't get along at first, but now they are best friends.
4
Give up
phrasal verb
▼

to stop doing or trying to do something; to stop trying to achieve something.

Example · Don't give up — keep practising and you will improve!
5
Keep up
phrasal verb
▼

to continue at the same pace or level; to not fall behind others.

Example · She ran as fast as she could to keep up with the rest of the team.
6
Pass on
phrasal verb
▼

to give or tell something to another person; to transmit information, knowledge, or objects.

Example · Could you pass on this message to the teacher, please?
7
Set up
phrasal verb
▼

to start or create something new, such as a business, organisation, or system.

Example · They decided to set up a study group to prepare for the exams.
8
Sign up
phrasal verb
▼

to officially join or register for something, such as a course, team, or service.

Example · I want to sign up for the after-school drama club this term.
9
Turn into
phrasal verb
▼

to change and become something different; to transform from one thing to another.

Example · The caterpillar will eventually turn into a beautiful butterfly.

📖 Vocabulary #5

Personality Adjectives II
12 words · Cambridge Dictionary Definitions · Character Traits
🗣️ Speaking Unit · 2026
💡 How to study this vocabulary: These adjectives describe personality traits. Study each definition and example sentence, then try to use at least 5 of these words to describe yourself or someone you know!
1
AFFECTIONATE
/əˈfek.ʃən.ət/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Showing feelings of liking or love for someone in a gentle and kind way.
💬 Example · She is very affectionate with her younger siblings — always hugging them.
💛 warm 🤗 loving ❌ Opposite: cold
2
SENSITIVE
/ˈsen.sɪ.tɪv/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Able to understand the feelings of other people and easily affected emotionally by what people say or do.
💬 Example · He is so sensitive that even small comments can hurt his feelings.
🧠 empathetic ❌ Opposite: insensitive
3
SENSIBLE
/ˈsen.sɪ.bl̩/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Showing good judgement and reason; practical and wise. ⚠️ Not the same as "sensitive"!
💬 Example · It was sensible of her to study in advance instead of waiting for the last day.
🧩 reasonable 💡 practical ❌ Opposite: foolish
4
OPEN-MINDED
/ˌəʊ.pənˈmaɪn.dɪd/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Willing to consider ideas and opinions that are new or different from your own.
💬 Example · A good teacher is open-minded and always listens to different perspectives.
🌍 tolerant ❌ Opposite: narrow-minded
5
BRIGHT
/braɪt/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Clever and quick to learn; intelligent.
💬 Example · She is a bright student who always understands new concepts quickly.
🧠 clever ✨ sharp ❌ Opposite: dull
6
CHARMING
/ˈtʃɑː.mɪŋ/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Very pleasing or attractive in a way that makes people like you.
💬 Example · Everyone liked the new student — he was incredibly charming and funny.
😊 delightful ❌ Opposite: unpleasant
7
ENTHUSIASTIC
/ɪnˌθjuː.ziˈæs.tɪk/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Feeling or showing a lot of excitement and interest about something.
💬 Example · The team was enthusiastic about the project and worked extra hours to finish it.
🔥 passionate ⚡ eager ❌ Opposite: apathetic
8
AMBITIOUS
/æmˈbɪʃ.əs/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Having a strong desire to be successful, powerful, or rich.
💬 Example · She is very ambitious and wants to become a doctor by the time she is 25.
🎯 driven 💪 determined ❌ Opposite: unmotivated
9
SOCIABLE
/ˈsəʊ.ʃə.bl̩/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Friendly and enjoying spending time with other people.
💬 Example · He is very sociable and makes friends wherever he goes.
🤝 outgoing 👥 friendly ❌ Opposite: withdrawn
10
SELF-CONFIDENT
/ˌself ˈkɒn.fɪ.dənt/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Feeling sure about your own ability to do things and be successful.
💬 Example · She walked into the interview self-confident and answered every question without hesitation.
💎 assured 🦁 bold ❌ Opposite: insecure
11
RESPECTFUL
/rɪˈspekt.fəl/ · adjective
▼
📖 Definition · Showing polite feelings of admiration and care towards other people.
💬 Example · The students were very respectful during the assembly, listening quietly.
🙏 polite 🎓 considerate ❌ Opposite: rude
12
TEAM PLAYER
/ˈtiːm ˌpleɪ.ər/ · noun phrase
▼
📖 Definition · Someone who works well as part of a group and puts the group's goals before their own.
💬 Example · We need a team player for this project — someone who collaborates well with others.
🤝 cooperative 🏆 collaborative ❌ Opposite: self-centered

Use Cambridge Dictionary online to listen to the pronunciation of each word. Then try to describe yourself or a classmate using at least 5 of these adjectives!
