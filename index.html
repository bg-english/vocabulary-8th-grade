Informative Article
12 words · Journalism & Media Vocabulary
📰 Writing & Speaking Unit
💡 How to study: Read the definition, study the example sentence, then check the synonyms and ↔ opposites. Try writing your own sentence with at least 5 words!
1 ARTICLE noun ▼
📖 Definition · A piece of writing on a particular subject published in a newspaper, magazine, or website.
💬 Example · I read an interesting article about climate change in the school newspaper.
📰 piece✍️ feature📄 report↔ ↔ social-media post
2 INTERVIEW noun / verb ▼
📖 Definition · A meeting in which one person asks another questions, especially for a newspaper, TV programme, or job.
💬 Example · The reporter conducted a live interview with the award-winning scientist.
🎤 questioning📋 meeting💬 dialogue↔ ↔ monologue
3 REPORT noun ▼
📖 Definition · A spoken or written account of a situation or event, usually made after careful study of the facts.
💬 Example · The journalist wrote a detailed report about the flooding in the city.
📋 account📊 summary📝 write-up↔ ↔ rumour
4 REPORTER noun ▼
📖 Definition · A person whose job is to find out about news events and describe them for a newspaper, TV, or radio.
💬 Example · The reporter rushed to the scene of the accident to cover the story.
📰 journalist🎤 correspondent📡 anchor↔ ↔ audience
5 HEADLINE noun ▼
📖 Definition · The title printed in large letters above a newspaper story, or the main news points broadcast on TV or radio.
💬 Example · The president's resignation was the headline on every newspaper.
📌 title📢 lead story🔖 banner↔ ↔ footnote
6 REVIEW noun ▼
📖 Definition · An article that gives an opinion about a new book, film, TV programme, restaurant, or other product.
💬 Example · The film received excellent reviews from critics around the world.
⭐ critique💬 opinion📝 evaluation↔ ↔ no comment
7 RESEARCH noun ▼
📖 Definition · A detailed study of a subject, especially to discover new information or reach new understanding.
💬 Example · She spent months doing research for her article on ancient civilisations.
🔍 investigation📚 study🧪 inquiry↔ ↔ guesswork
8 FIND OUT phrasal verb ▼
📖 Definition · To get information about something; to learn a fact or piece of news for the first time.
💬 Example · The reporter tried to find out what really happened at the event.
🔎 discover💡 learn📖 uncover↔ ↔ stay ignorant
9 TIMELINE noun ▼
📖 Definition · A diagram or plan showing the order in which events happened or are expected to happen.
💬 Example · The teacher drew a timeline to show the key events of World War II.
📅 schedule⏱️ sequence🗓️ chronology↔ ↔ random order
10 STATEMENT noun ▼
📖 Definition · Something said or written officially, expressing a fact, opinion, or point of view.
💬 Example · The company made an official statement about the new policy.
📣 announcement💬 declaration📄 comment↔ ↔ silence
11 NEWSWORTHY adjective ▼
📖 Definition · Interesting or important enough to be reported in the news.
💬 Example · The discovery of water on Mars was considered extremely newsworthy.
📰 important🔥 significant⚡ notable↔ ↔ trivial
12 DISINTEREST noun ▼
📖 Definition · A lack of interest in something or someone.
💬 Example · His disinterest in the topic was obvious from the way he looked out the window.
😐 indifference💤 apathy😑 boredom↔ ↔ enthusiasm
